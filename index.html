I am broadly interested in problems related to the scattering of seismic energy. Seismic scattering is the process by which small scale heterogeneities cause coherent wave fronts to randomly disperse. The concentration and size distribution of the scatterers will control the intensity of the scattering and the frequencies that are affected. A high level of scattering will generate coda waves in the recorded seismic signals and will generally obscure useful secondary wave arrivals that could be used to investigate the interior of a planetary body. This effect is especially strong on the Moon. My PhD research aims at better understanding and characterizing the seismic scattering in the lunar interior. I work under the supervision of Prof. Catherine Johnson. I am also interested in the acquisition and processing of orbital geophysical data of all kind. I spent a summer as an intern at the Lunar and Planetary Institute (Houston, TX) putting together a GIS database which includes most of the publicly available lunar orbital datasets. The purpose of this database was to facilitate the selection of lunar mission landing sites based on the National Research Council lunar research priorities.
